Useful information

A lot of the food we throw away is still perfectly edible. For example, the “best before” date label attached to most products can be misleading, and only means the food has the best quality before the date that is mentioned. This doesn’t necessarily mean that it should not be eaten. In most cases it will be perfectly fine for a lot longer.

Just by being aware of a couple of things, we can avoid throwing away an impressive amount of food, potentially saving a lot of money and helping the environment.

Taken from an article published on forskning.no, here are a few tips we should know about:

  • First of all, we should use our senses before we decide to throw something away. Look at the food, smell and taste it. It is up to us to determine if food can still be eaten.
  • Dairy products like yogurt can last many weeks after the best before date has passed, as long as the packaging hasn’t been opened. Cheese for instance can often get even better while it is stored longer. Just put a piece of it in a closed plastic bag if the packaging can not be closed. Mold can be sliced off.

    • Exampel of protein on the sides of the cheese (source)

  • Products like sugar, flour and pasta is edible for years after its expiration date.
  • Crispbread (Knekkebrød) is edible for a lot longer than the expiration date claims. Bread is easily conservable, even if it is out of date. You just need to follow a few steps. First, the bread needs to be placed in cold water for a couple of seconds. Then it should be warmed in the oven at 200 degrees Celsius for about 10 minutes. The bread will be almost as good as new. However, if the bread is moldy, it should be disposed of.
  • Red meat often just gets better and better when you store it, but if the meat is of a grayish brown color, slimy or smells bad, you should throw it away. You should be careful with white meat, like for instance chicken. The same goes for ground meat, sausages and fresh fish. These products will be marked with an expiration date (not "best before"), and should not be used after this date. However, if the product has been frozen, this date will no longer hold meaning, and you should use your senses when deciding wether or not to use it.
